1. What is FnB?

FnB (Fetch and Build) is a web-based platform that digitizes the student project evaluation process. Instead of scattered emails and handwritten notes, everything happens in one place — students submit projects, teachers review and give structured feedback, and administrators manage accounts.

📤

Project Submission

Students upload their work with metadata like tech stack, innovation level, and a project file.

🔍

Smart Filtering

Teachers filter projects by technology, innovation, or review status to quickly find what they need.

💬

Structured Feedback

Teachers write remarks, suggestions, and improvement notes — all stored and linked to the right student.

🔒

Secure & Private

Each student sees only their own feedback. Role-based access keeps every user in their lane.

🌐

2. Accessing FnB

FnB runs in a web browser. No installation needed. Open any modern browser (Google Chrome recommended) and go to:

http://localhost:5000
⚙ FnB is running. You will be redirected to the Login page automatically.
ℹ️
Default URL The address http://localhost:5000 is for running on your local computer. If your teacher has hosted it on a server, they will give you the actual web address to use.

If you are not logged in, FnB automatically redirects you to the login page. If you are already logged in, it takes you straight to your role's dashboard.

✍️

5. Student Dashboard

Your dashboard is the first page you see after logging in. It shows a summary of all your submitted projects.

📁

Total Projects

Total number of projects you have submitted so far.

⏳

Pending Review

Projects awaiting teacher feedback — shown with an orange "Pending" badge.

✅

Reviewed

Projects your teacher has already given feedback on — shown with a green "Reviewed" badge.

Each project card shows the title, tech stack, innovation level, quality rating (once given), submission date, and a View Feedback button (appears only after the teacher reviews it).

📤

6. Submitting a Project

Click Submit New Project on your dashboard or Submit Project in the nav bar.

1

Enter Project Title

Give your project a clear, descriptive name. E.g. "Smart Traffic Management System".

2

Write a Description

Explain what your project does, its goals, and how it works. This is what the teacher reads first.

3

Enter Tech Stack

List the languages, frameworks, and tools you used. E.g. "Python, Flask, MongoDB, HTML, CSS".

4

Fill in USP (optional)

What makes your project unique? E.g. "Uses AI-based prediction to reduce waiting time by 40%".

5

Select Innovation Level

Pick Low, Medium, or High based on how original your idea is compared to standard implementations.

6

Upload a File (optional)

Attach your project file — PDF report, ZIP of code, screenshots, etc.

📎 Allowed file types: PDF, ZIP, PNG, JPG, TXT, PY, HTML, DOCX. Max size: 16 MB.
7

Click "Submit Project"

You'll see a green success message and be taken back to your dashboard with the new project listed as "Pending".

FieldRequired?Description
Project TitleRequiredShort, clear name for your project (max 100 chars)
DescriptionRequiredFull explanation of your project (max 2000 chars)
Tech StackRequiredTechnologies used, separated by commas
USPOptionalUnique selling point / what makes your project special
Innovation LevelRequiredLow / Medium / High — defaults to Medium
Project FileOptionalAttachment for teacher to download and review

7. Viewing Your Feedback

Once your teacher reviews a project, you can read their feedback. There are two ways to access it:

A

From the Dashboard

On any project card with a green "Reviewed" badge, click the View Feedback button.

B

From the Nav Bar

Click My Feedback in the navigation to see feedback for all your reviewed projects at once.

Each feedback card shows:

SectionWhat it Means
💬 RemarksGeneral evaluation of your project from the teacher
✅ SuggestionsSpecific things to improve or add to your project
🔧 Improvement NotesDetailed guidance on how to make your project better
⭐ Quality RatingA score from 1 to 5 stars assigned by the teacher
📅 DateWhen feedback was given, and if it was updated later
💡
Privacy guarantee You can only ever see feedback for your own projects. You will never see another student's feedback, even if you try to access it directly.

10. Filtering Projects

Above the projects table, there is a filter bar. You can narrow down projects by:

🛠️

Tech Stack

Type any technology (e.g. "Flask" or "React") to find all projects using it. Partial matches work.

💡

Innovation Level

Select Low, Medium, or High from the dropdown to filter by the student's self-reported innovation.

📌

Status

Show only "Pending" (not yet reviewed) or "Reviewed" projects.

1

Enter filter values in the bar

Fill in one or more filter fields. You can combine them — e.g. "Python" + "High" shows all high-innovation Python projects.

2

Click "Apply Filters"

The table updates to show only matching projects. The count at the top shows how many were found.

3

Click "Clear" to reset

Removes all filters and shows the full list again.

✏️

11. Giving Feedback

Click Give Feedback next to any project. Fill in the form and submit.

1

Click "Details" on a project

This opens the full project page where you can read the description and see any uploaded file.

2

Click "Give Feedback"

The feedback form opens. The project summary is shown at the top as a quick reminder.

3

Write Remarks (required)

This is your overall evaluation. At least this field must be filled in before submitting.

4

Add Suggestions & Improvement Notes (optional)

Give specific advice on what to improve and detailed guidance notes for the student.

5

Set a Quality Rating (optional)

Choose 1–5 stars. This is visible to the student on their project card.

6

Click "Submit Feedback"

The feedback is saved, the project status changes to "Reviewed", and the student can now see it.

FieldRequired?Description
RemarksRequiredGeneral evaluation of the project (max 2000 chars)
SuggestionsOptionalSpecific improvements the student should make
Improvement NotesOptionalDetailed step-by-step guidance for the student
Quality RatingOptional1 (Needs Major Work) to 5 (Excellent)
ℹ️
Editing feedback If you want to change feedback you already gave, go back to the project and click Edit Feedback. The form will be pre-filled with your previous comments. Your changes overwrite the old feedback and an "Updated" timestamp is shown.

14. First-Time Setup (Creating the Admin Account)

Admin accounts cannot be created through the normal Register page. The very first admin must be created manually by running a script once. After that, admins can create more admins from inside the app.

⚠️
One-time step only This only needs to be done once, when the system is first set up. After the admin account exists, all future management happens inside FnB.
1

Make sure the app and MongoDB are running

Start MongoDB (mongod) and run python app.py in the project folder.

2

Open a Python shell in the project folder

In a new terminal window, type python and press Enter.

3

Run the seed script

Copy and paste this code into the Python shell:

from app import app
from db import mongo
import bcrypt
from datetime import datetime

with app.app_context():
  pw = bcrypt.hashpw(b'admin123', bcrypt.gensalt(rounds=10))
  mongo.db.users.insert_one({
    'username': 'admin',
    'password_hash': pw,
    'role': 'admin',
    'email': 'admin@fnb.com',
    'is_active': True,
    'created_at': datetime.utcnow()
  })
  print("Admin created!")
4

Login with the new admin credentials

Go to http://localhost:5000/login and use Username: admin  |  Password: admin123

🔒 Change this password immediately after first login using "Change Password" in the nav bar.
👥

15. Managing Users

Go to Manage Users from the nav bar. This page has two parts:

15.1 Create a New User

The form at the top of the page lets you create any type of user directly without them needing to register themselves.

1

Fill in username, email, password, and role

Pick the role: Student, Teacher, or Admin. This is the only way to create admin accounts.

2

Click "Create User"

The user appears immediately in the table below and can log in right away.

15.2 User Management Table

The table shows all registered users with their details. You can take two actions on each user:

ActionHowEffect
Change Role Select a new role from the dropdown and click Set The user's role updates immediately. They will have different permissions on their next login.
Disable Account Click the red Disable button The user can no longer log in. Their data is preserved. Click Enable to restore access.
⚠️
You cannot disable your own account The system prevents admins from accidentally locking themselves out. The buttons are greyed out on your own row.

17. Logging Out

Click the red Logout button in the top-right corner of the navigation bar. You can log out at any time from any page.

After logout, your session is completely cleared. You'll be redirected to the Login page with a confirmation message. Anyone who uses the same computer after you won't have access to your account.

ℹ️
Always log out on shared computers If you're using a shared or public computer, always click Logout when you're done to protect your account.

18. Frequently Asked Questions

❓ I registered but can't log in — what's wrong?

Check your username and password carefully (they are case-sensitive). If your account was recently created by an admin, confirm with them that it is enabled. If you keep failing, ask your admin to reset your password.

❓ I submitted a project but I don't see it on my dashboard

Refresh the page. If it still doesn't appear, check that you were logged in as a Student (not Teacher or Admin) when you submitted. Projects are tied to the account that was logged in at submission time.

❓ My teacher gave feedback but I can't see it

Go to My Feedback in the nav bar. If it's not there, the teacher may not have submitted feedback yet (the project status on your dashboard would still show "Pending").

❓ Can I edit my project after submitting?

No. Once submitted, project details cannot be changed. If you need to make corrections, ask your admin or teacher — they may advise you to submit a new version.

❓ Can I see other students' projects or feedback?

No. Students can only see their own projects and feedback. This is enforced at the database level — it's not just hidden, it's technically inaccessible.

❓ The flash message (green/red notification) appeared for a second and disappeared

Flash messages auto-dismiss after 4 seconds. You can also click the ✕ button on the right to dismiss them manually. They appear at the top of the page just below the navigation bar.

❓ I uploaded a file but the teacher says they can't open it

Make sure the file is one of the supported types: PDF, ZIP, PNG, JPG, TXT, PY, HTML, or DOCX. If the file is too large (over 16 MB), the upload may have silently failed. Try compressing or splitting the file.

❓ The dark mode button (🌙) is in the nav bar — what does it do?

It switches the site between a light background and a dark background. Click it to toggle. Your preference is saved in the browser so it remembers your choice next time.
